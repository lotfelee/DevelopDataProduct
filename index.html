Studying the effect of Manual gear vs Automatic transmission on fuel consumbtion

Summary:

Motor Trend, a magazine about the automobile industry. Looking at a data set of a collection of cars, they are interested in exploring the relationship between a set of variables and miles per gallon (MPG) (outcome). They are particularly interested in the following two questions:

1 . Is an automatic or manual transmission better for MPG

2 . Quantify the MPG difference between automatic and manual transmissions

My strategy as below:

  • Regress mpg to am only .
  • Regress mpg to all another variables .
  • Regress mpg to the most correlated variables with mpg .

Finally choosing the best model using root mean square error of the models to answer the latter questions .

Is an automatic or manual transmission better for MPG

When i regress the mpg to all the another variables i got an "am" Coefficient value of 2.52 , When i regress the mpg to "am" only i got an "am" Coefficient value of 7.24 , when i regress the mpg to only the highly correlated variables with mpg meaning(cyl+disp+hp+wt+carb+am) i got 1.9 . bottom line in the three cases it is above zero meaning when am=1 (manual gear) on average will always get an increase in mpg ,Manual is better for mpg ,and the below box plot also support this deduction.

Quantify the MPG difference between automatic and manual transmissions

to answer this i have to choose a model between my three last models to do so i calcualated the root mean square error for the three model and i chose the model with the lower rmse:

RMS for model1 is 2.15 ,RMS for model2 is 4.75 and RMS for model3 is 2.25 So based on RMS values for the three models i chose model1 where i regress mpg to all another variables .
